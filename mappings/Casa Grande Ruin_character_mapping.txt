	*Casa Grande	Nezumi	*female	2. A large adobe ruin structure located near the southwestern corner of the Casa Grande group, built in a late period of Pueblo occupation. The structure is characterized by its three central rooms arranged side by side, with two additional rooms at the northern and southern ends. It features three stories in its central room, with walls reaching 28-30 feet above ground level. The ruin is notable for its well-preserved walls and unique construction methods using rammed earth blocks, though it lacks any trace of molded adobe brick. The structure was built in the late prehistoric period and represents one of the best preserved examples of a type of architecture found primarily in the Gila valley.
