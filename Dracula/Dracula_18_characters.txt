Dr. Seward	male	A physician and diary keeper who is the primary narrator of the chapter. He runs a psychiatric asylum and is deeply involved in treating patients like Mr. Renfield. He is methodical, observant, and committed to his medical duties.
Mrs. Harker	female	The wife of Jonathan Harker, a strong and compassionate woman who is deeply involved in the investigation. She has been keeping detailed records of events and shows great courage despite her fear.
Mr. Renfield	*male	A patient in Dr. Seward's asylum who is described as having a 'pronounced' mental condition, but also displays moments of apparent sanity and philosophical insight. He has a complex relationship with life and death, particularly regarding consuming living things.
Lord Godalming	male	A wealthy and nobleman who is involved in the investigation of the vampire threat. He has a strong sense of duty and honor, particularly for Lucy's sake.
Mr. Morris	male	A friend of the group who is practical and direct in his approach. He has a history with bats, which he fears greatly due to recent events.
Professor Van Helsing	*male	An elderly, wise, and knowledgeable expert on the supernatural who is leading the investigation against Dracula. He has extensive knowledge of vampire lore and traditions.
Jonathan Harker	male	Mrs. Harker's husband who has recently returned from his journey to Transylvania and is deeply involved in the investigation. He is described as brave, self-reliant, and resolute.
