Mina Harker	female	Jonathan Harker's wife, a kind and devoted woman who keeps a journal and is deeply concerned about her husband's wellbeing. She is intelligent, trustworthy, and shows great strength in facing adversity.
Jonathan Harker	male	Mina's husband, a solicitor who has experienced traumatic events in Transylvania involving the Count. He is initially suffering from brain fever and shock but later recovers with help from Van Helsing.
Abraham Van Helsing	male	A Dutch doctor and expert in the supernatural, friend of Dr. Seward and Lord Godalming. He is wise, kind-hearted, and possesses extensive knowledge about the occult and monsters.
Lucy Westenra	female	A young woman who died from a mysterious illness, described as having been bitten by something that caused punctured wounds on her throat. She was a close friend of Mina and had sleep-walking episodes.
Arthur Holmwood	*male	Lucy's fiancé and later husband, who is deeply affected by Lucy's death. He is described as being in a state of despair and is helped by Quincey Morris.
Quincey Morris	male	A friend of Arthur Holmwood who provides support during his time of grief. He is described as having a 'bubbling well of good spirits' and helps Arthur recover.
Dr. John Seward	male	A physician and friend of Van Helsing who works with him to investigate supernatural events. He is a colleague of Van Helsing and Arthur Holmwood.
Mary	*female	The Harkers' housemaid who serves as a helper in their household.
Renfield	*male	A patient under Dr. Seward's care who becomes increasingly erratic and is later described as having a connection to the supernatural events.
Count	*male	The vampire antagonist who has moved to London and is responsible for the deaths of Lucy Westenra and other children. He is described as having supernatural powers.
