Mina Murray	female	The narrator of the journal entries, a young woman who is deeply caring and devoted to her friend Lucy Westenra. She is practical, observant, and shows great concern for others' wellbeing.
Lucy Westenra	female	A young woman who is the focus of much care and attention in the story. She experiences sleepwalking episodes, appears to be developing a mysterious illness, and is engaged to Arthur Holmwood.
Arthur Holmwood	*male	Lucy's fiancé, who is mentioned as being very important to Lucy and whose father is reportedly better. He is described by Lucy as someone she loves.
Mrs. Westenra	female	Lucy's mother, who is described as being ill and whose health is a concern for Mina. She has been told she will die within months.
Jonathan Harker	*male	Mina's fiancé who has been ill in Budapest and is recovering. He was traveling to his home country when he became seriously unwell.
Sister Agatha	female	A nun at the hospital in Budapest where Jonathan Harker was being treated. She writes a letter to Mina about his condition and recovery.
Dr. Seward	*male	A physician who keeps a diary and is involved in treating patients at an asylum. He has particular interest in Renfield.
Renfield	*male	A patient at the asylum who exhibits strange behavior and religious mania. He becomes dangerous when he escapes.
Peter Hawkins	*male	A man who is mentioned in a letter from Jonathan Harker to Mina. He appears to be involved with the care of Jonathan.
Mr. Swales	*male	A character mentioned by Lucy in a playful reference to her sleepwalking experience. He is described as an old man who would have told Lucy about the sound of her feet.
Geordie	*male	A character mentioned by Lucy in a playful reference to her sleepwalking experience. He is described as someone who would have been woken by Lucy's footsteps.
