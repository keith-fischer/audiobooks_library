Jim	male	A enslaved man who travels with the narrator on the Mississippi River. He believes in superstitions and is superstitious about bad luck, particularly regarding snakes and unburied corpses. He is practical yet fearful of supernatural consequences.
*Pap	*male	The narrator's father, a drunkard who is mentioned as having given Jim whisky to treat his snake bite. He is described negatively and is associated with alcoholism.
*Old Hank Bunker	*male	A local man who was known for looking at the new moon over his left shoulder, which led to a tragic accident where he fell from a shot tower and was killed.
*The woman	female	A stranger living in a neglected shanty on the Illinois shore. She is knitting by candlelight and appears to be unfamiliar with the local area.
