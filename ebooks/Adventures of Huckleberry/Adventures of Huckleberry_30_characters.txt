the king	male	The elderly monarch who is easily angered and prone to violence, particularly towards the narrator. He accuses the narrator of trying to leave them behind and becomes furious when confronted about hiding money in a coffin. He is ultimately revealed as the one who hid the money but initially denies it.
the duke	male	The cunning and manipulative companion who is more calculating than the king. He defends himself against accusations of hiding money, accuses the king of being dishonest and greedy, and ultimately gets into a heated argument with him over who actually hid the money. He is also prone to drinking heavily.
Jim	*male	A loyal companion who supports the narrator's story. He is described as being afraid for his own safety and relieved when he sees the king and duke return. He is a key witness in confirming the narrator's account.
