	narrator	When they got aboard the king went for me, and shook me by the collar, and says:
	the king	Tryin’ to give us the slip, was ye, you pup! Tired of our company, hey?
	narrator	I says: No, your majesty, we warn’t—_please_ don't, your majesty!
	the king	Quick, then, and tell us what _was_ your idea, or I’ll shake the insides out o’
	the king	you!
	narrator	Honest, I’ll tell you everything just as it happened, your majesty. The man that had a-holt of me was very good to me,
	narrator	and kept saying he had a boy about as big as me that died last year,
	narrator	and he was sorry to see a boy in such a dangerous fix; and when they was all took by surprise by finding the gold,
	narrator	and made a rush for the coffin, he lets go of me and whispers, ‘Heel it now,
	narrator	or they’ll hang ye, sure!’ and I lit out. It didn’t seem no good for _me_ to stay—_I_ couldn't do nothing,
	narrator	and I didn’t want to be hung if I could get away. So I never stopped running till I found the canoe; and when I got here I told Jim to hurry,
	narrator	or they’d catch me and hang me yet, and said I was afeard you and the duke wasn’t alive now,
	narrator	and I was awful sorry, and so was Jim, and was awful glad when we see you coming; you may ask Jim if I didn’t.
	jim	It was so;
	the king	And the king told him to shut up, and said, "Oh, yes, it's _mighty_ likely!" and shook me up again,
	the king	and said he reckoned he'd drownd me. But the duke says:
	the duke	Leggo the boy, you old idiot! Would _you_ a done any different? Did you inquire around for _him_ when you got loose?
	the duke	_I_ don't remember it.
	narrator	So the king let go of me, and begun to cuss that town and everybody in it.
	narrator	But the duke says:
	the duke	You better a blame sight give _yourself_ a good cussing, for you're the one that's entitled to it most.
	the duke	You hain't done a thing from the start that had any sense in it, except coming out so cool and cheeky with that imaginary blue-arrow mark.
	the duke	That _was_ bright—it was right down bully; and it was the thing that saved us. For if it hadn't been for that,
	the duke	they'd a jailed us till them Englishmen's baggage come—and then—the penitentiary, you bet! But that trick took 'em to the graveyard,
	the duke	and the gold done us a still bigger kindness; for if the excited fools hadn't let go all holts and made that rush to get a look,
	the duke	we'd a slept in our cravats to-night—cravats warranted to _wear_, too—longer than _we'd_ need 'em.
	narrator	They was still a minute—thinking; then the king says, kind of absent-minded like:
	the king	Mf! And we reckoned the _niggers_ stole it!
	narrator	That made me squirm!
	the king	Yes,
	the duke	kinder slow and deliberate and sarcastic, "_We_ did.
	narrator	After about a half a minute the king drawls out:
	the king	Leastways, _I_ did.
	the duke	On the contrary, _I_ did.
	the king	Looky here, Bilgewater, what'r you referrin' to?
	the duke	When it comes to that, maybe you'll let me ask, what was _you_ referring to?
	the king	Shucks! but _I_ don't know—maybe you was asleep, and didn't know what you was about.
	the duke	Oh, let _up_ on this cussed nonsense; do you take me for a blame' fool? Don't you reckon _I_ know who hid that money in that coffin?
	the king	Yes, sir! I know you _do_ know, because you done it yourself!
	the duke	It's a lie!
	narrator	—and the duke went for him. The king sings out:
	the king	Take y'r hands off!—leggo my throat!—I take it all back!
	the duke	Well, you just own up, first, that you _did_ hide that money there, intending to give me the slip one of these days,
	the duke	and come back and dig it up, and have it all to yourself.
	the king	Wait jest a minute, duke—answer me this one question, honest and fair; if you didn't put the money there,
	the king	say it, and I'll b'lieve you, and take back everything I said.
	the duke	You old scoundrel, I didn't, and you know I didn't. There, now!
	the king	Well, then, I b'lieve you. But answer me only jest this one more—now_don't_ git mad; didn't you have it in your mind to hook the money and hide it?
	the duke	Well, I don't care if I _did_, I didn't _do_ it, anyway. But you not only had it in mind to do it,
	the duke	but you _done_ it.
	the king	I wisht I never die if I done it, duke, and that's honest. I won't say I warn't _goin'_ to do it,
	the king	because I _was_; but you—I mean somebody—got in ahead o' me.
	the duke	It's a lie! You done it, and you got to _say_ you done it, or—
	narrator	The king began to gurgle, and then he gasps out:
	the king	Nough!—_I own up!_
	narrator	I was very glad to hear him say that; it made me feel much more easier than what I was feeling before.
	narrator	So the duke took his hands off and says:
	the duke	If you ever deny it again I'll drown you. It's _well_ for you to set there and blubber like a baby—it's fitten for you,
	the duke	after the way you've acted. I never see such an old ostrich for wanting to gobble everything—and I a-trusting you all the time,
	the duke	like you was my own father. You ought to been ashamed of yourself to stand by and hear it saddled on to a lot of poor niggers,
	the duke	and you never say a word for 'em. It makes me feel ridiculous to think I was soft enough to _believe_ that rubbage.
	the duke	Cuss you, I can see now why you was so anxious to make up the deffisit—you wanted to get what money I'd got out of the Nonesuch and one thing or another,
	the duke	and scoop it _all!_
	the king	Why, duke, it was you that said make up the deffisit; it warn't me.
	the duke	Dry up! I don't want to hear no more _out_ of you! And _now_ you see what you _got_ by it.
	the duke	They've got all their own money back, and all of _ourn_ but a shekel or two _besides_.
	the duke	G'long to bed, and don't you deffersit _me_ no more deffersits, long 's _you_ live!
	narrator	So the king sneaked into the wigwam and took to his bottle for comfort, and before long the duke tackled his bottle; and so in about a half an hour they was as thick as thieves again,
	narrator	and the tighter they got, the lovinger they got, and went off a-snoring in each other's arms.
	narrator	They both got powerful mellow, but I noticed the king didn't get mellow enough to forget to remember to not deny about hiding the money-bag again.
	narrator	That made me feel easy and satisfied. Of course when they got to snoring we had a long gabble,
	narrator	and I told Jim everything.
