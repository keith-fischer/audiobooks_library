Sir Tristram	male	A noble knight of Lyonesse, son of King Meliodas and Queen Elizabeth. Known for his strength, skill in battle, and tragic love for La Belle Isault. He fought Sir Marhaus of Ireland, overcame Sir Palomedes at a tournament, and was involved in many adventures including his time in Ireland where he was betrayed by the queen. He is a key figure in Arthurian legend, known for his valor and honor.
King Arthur	male	The legendary king of Britain, ruler of Camelot and leader of the Round Table. He holds high festivals at Caerleon and is a central figure in Arthurian legend, known for his wisdom, justice, and chivalry.
Sir Lancelot	male	A renowned knight of the Round Table, known as one of the greatest knights in Arthurian legend. He is described as peerless in courtesy and knighthood, and his name carries great respect among knights.
*Queen Guinevere	female	The queen of King Arthur's court, wife to the king. She is present at Sir Tristram's return and is part of the courtly society that welcomes him.
King Mark	male	The king of Cornwall and uncle to Sir Tristram. He is initially welcoming but later becomes jealous of Tristram's fame and favors, eventually plotting against him.
La Belle Isault	*female	The daughter of King Anguish of Ireland, later married to King Mark. She is described as fair and noble, and becomes the object of Sir Tristram's love and devotion.
*Sir Marhaus	male	A knight of the Round Table and brother to Queen Isault. He is defeated by Sir Tristram in a battle for the Cornish tribute, and dies from his wounds.
