Lucy Westenra	female	The main female character, a young woman who is ill and being treated by Dr. Van Helsing and Dr. Seward. She is described as loving, peaceful, and vulnerable to supernatural forces. She keeps a diary and records her experiences with sleepwalking and the vampire threat.
Dr. Van Helsing	male	A Dutch doctor who is treating Lucy and investigating the supernatural events. He is knowledgeable, determined, and appears to be a key figure in combating the vampire threat.
*Dr. Seward	male	A doctor who is treating Lucy and keeping detailed diaries of the events. He is observant, professional, and becomes increasingly distressed by what he witnesses.
Mrs. Westenra	female	Lucy's mother, who is described as an early riser and concerned about her daughter's health. She removes the garlic flowers from Lucy's room, inadvertently causing harm.
*Arthur	*male	Lucy's fiancé, mentioned as being important to her and to whom she writes a farewell note. He is described as dear to Lucy.
Thomas Bilder	*male	The keeper of the wolf section at the Zoölogical Gardens. He is described as elderly, hospitable, and somewhat grumpy but good-natured.
Mrs. Tom	female	Thomas Bilder's wife, who is described as elderly and hospitable. She laughs at her husband's jokes and helps him with his work.
*Bersicker	*male	A grey wolf from Norway that escaped from the Zoölogical Gardens. He is described as a 'nice well-behaved wolf' who later returns to his cage.
*The Keeper	*male	The reporter who interviews Thomas Bilder about the escaped wolf. He is described as a journalist from the Pall Mall Gazette.
*The Patient	*male	A patient of Dr. Seward who attacks him with a dinner-knife in the study, showing signs of being under some kind of supernatural influence.
*The Maids	*female	Several female servants who work in the house. They are described as frightened, nervous, and eventually drugged by an unknown person.
