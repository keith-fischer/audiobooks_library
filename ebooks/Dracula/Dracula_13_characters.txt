Dr. Seward	male	A physician and diary keeper who is deeply involved in the supernatural events surrounding Lucy Westenra's death. He is a close friend of Van Helsing and Arthur Holmwood, and serves as the primary narrator of much of the story.
Van Helsing	male	A Dutch physician and professor who is a key figure in investigating the supernatural events. He is described as being both a doctor and lawyer, and shows deep concern for Lucy's case.
*Lucy Westenra	female	A young woman who becomes a vampire and dies, causing great sorrow to her family. She is described as extremely beautiful both in life and death.
Arthur Holmwood	male	Lucy's fiancé who is deeply affected by her death. He is described as a devoted and loving man, and later becomes the owner of Lucy's estate.
*Mrs. Westenra	female	Lucy's mother who dies before Lucy, leaving her estate to Arthur Holmwood.
*Quincey Morris	male	A friend of Arthur who accompanies him back to Ring after Lucy's death. He is described as a fine fellow and moral Viking.
Mina Harker	female	Jonathan Harker's wife who keeps a journal and is involved in the supernatural events. She is described as intelligent, well-educated, and devoted to her husband.
*Jonathan Harker	male	Mina's husband who is a solicitor and partner in his firm. He has been abroad on business and suffers from nervous problems.
Mr. Marquand	male	A solicitor who handles the legal affairs of Mrs. Westenra's estate and explains the testamentary disposition.
The Undertaker	*male	A professional who handles the funeral arrangements for Lucy and Mrs. Westenra, described as having a suavity about his work.
*The Woman	*female	A servant who shows devotion by watching alone by Lucy's bier, though her name is not given.
*The Dark Man	*male	A mysterious man with a beaky nose, black moustache and pointed beard who is observed by Jonathan Harker.
*The Pretty Girl	*female	A beautiful young woman in a cart-wheel hat who is observed by Jonathan Harker and the dark man.
*The Children	*male	Young children who have gone missing in Hampstead, claiming to have been with a 'bloofer lady' and showing wounds to their throats.
