David Balfour	*male	The protagonist of the story, a young man fleeing from his pursuers. He is described as having a 'hang-dog, rag-and-tatter, clappermaclaw kind of a look' and is portrayed as being in poor health and weary from his journey. He is the heir to an inheritance but is currently hunted as an outlaw.
Alan Breck	*male	David's companion and protector, a resourceful and cunning man who takes charge of their escape plan. He is described as having 'a great fund of roguishness' and is the one who orchestrates their scheme to obtain a boat. He speaks with authority and has a strong sense of responsibility for David's safety.
*Mr. Rankeillor	*male	A wealthy landowner and writer who is the destination of David's journey. He is described as a 'kennt man' and represents safety, wealth, and the fulfillment of David's inheritance claims.
The lass	*female	A kind-hearted servant girl working at the change-house in Limekilns. She is described as 'good-looking' and shows compassion for David's plight, ultimately helping them cross the Firth by providing a boat. She is portrayed as simple-minded but generous.
*The old hobbling woman	*female	An elderly woman who appears on the bridge at Stirling, described as 'so little' and with a crutch stick. She is the first person they encounter during their attempt to cross the bridge, and her presence delays their passage.
The maid	*female	A servant girl working at the change-house in Limekilns who is described as 'good-looking' and shows sympathy for David's condition. She becomes involved in Alan's scheme to obtain a boat.
