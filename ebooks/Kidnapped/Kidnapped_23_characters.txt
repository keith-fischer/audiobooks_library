Cluny	male	A fugitive Highland chief and outlaw, known as Cluny Macpherson, who lives in a hidden dwelling called 'Cluny's Cage' on Ben Alder. He is described as having the manners of a king despite his outlaw status, with precise habits like an old maid. He exercises patriarchal justice in his clan and is known for his gambling, having lost a large sum of money to Alan Breck in cards. He is proud and has a strong sense of honor, though he can be easily angered.
David Balfour	male	The narrator and protagonist, a young man who has recently arrived in Scotland. He is the Laird of Shaws and comes from a family with strong Presbyterian/Covenanting values. He is religiously opposed to gambling, which causes tension with Cluny and Alan. He is described as having a strong sense of honor and integrity, particularly in his dealings with Cluny.
Alan Breck	male	A friend of David Balfour who is described as a gentleman with a 'mettle' character. He has a strong sense of honor and loyalty, as shown by his willingness to defend David against Cluny. He is a gambler who loses money in cards at Cluny's house, which creates tension between him and David. He is described as having a 'Whiggish' outlook.
*The Barber-Gillie	*male	A gillie who serves Cluny and is both a barber and doctor. He comes to shave Cluny each morning and provides him with news from the country, which Cluny is 'immoderately greedy' to hear. He speaks in Gaelic and prescribes medicine for David.
*The Gillie	*male	A servant who works for Cluny and is questioned by him along with Alan. He brings news to Cluny and serves as a messenger between the outside world and Cluny's hiding place.
*Prince Charlie	male	The Young Pretender, son of James Francis Edward Stuart. He stayed in Cluny's Cage and is described as a gracious, spirited boy with the bearing of a race of polite kings. He was often drunk during his stay and showed early signs of the character flaws that would later affect him.
