Alan	*male	A Hieland gentleman and partner of Mr. Thomson, known for his cunning and threat-making tactics. He is the primary negotiator with Ebenezer Balfour regarding David's ransom and later reveals himself to be James Thomson, a man of noble birth who has been using his royal name as a means of leverage.
Ebenezer Balfour	*male	The uncle of David, a man of principle who initially refuses to pay a ransom for his nephew but eventually agrees to keep him. He is portrayed as a man of modest means who has been deceived by Hoseason and is ultimately forced to pay for his brother's son.
*David	*male	The nephew of Ebenezer Balfour, a young man who was found half-drowned on the sands and taken in by Alan's friends. He is being held for ransom, with his fate depending on whether Ebenezer chooses to pay or not.
*Hoseason	*male	A sailor-man who kidnapped David and was paid twenty pounds by Ebenezer Balfour. He is revealed to be a liar who told Alan that David was kidnapped, though Ebenezer denies this.
Rankeillor	*male	A lawyer who arrives at the scene and takes control of the situation. He is civil and professional, offering to make 'easy terms' for Ebenezer Balfour after Alan's threats. He is instrumental in finalizing the agreement between David and Ebenezer.
Torrance	*male	A character who joins the group after Alan's arrival. He is present during the negotiations and helps with the wine and supper, showing loyalty to Alan and David.
*Shaws	*male	The name of the estate or property that David is to inherit, which will provide him with a substantial income. It's mentioned in the agreement between David and his uncle.
