Alan Breck Stewart	male	A Highland clansman and former soldier in the English army who deserted to the Jacobite side. He is a member of the Appin Stewart clan, known for his swordsmanship and fierce loyalty to his chief Ardshiel. He has a deep hatred for the Campbell clan, particularly Colin Campbell of Glenure known as the Red Fox. He is described as skilled in music, poetry, fishing, and fencing.
David	male	The narrator of the story, a young man who is traveling with Alan Breck Stewart. He is described as a Whig and relatively inexperienced, being 'so young' but wiser than his thoughts suggest. He shows respect for Alan's character and is curious about Highland affairs.
*Duncan Stewart	*male	Alan Breck's father, a former soldier in the Black Watch who was known for his swordsmanship and had been chosen to demonstrate Highland sword skills before King George and Queen Caroline in London.
*Ardshiel	*male	The chief of the Appin Stewart clan, who was forced into exile after the defeat at Culloden. He is described as a man of great stature and noble blood, now living in France but maintaining his clan's loyalty.
*James Stewart	*male	A relative of Ardshiel and Alan's father's friend, who serves as the agent for collecting rents from the clan members to support Ardshiel in exile. He is described as a man who manages the financial affairs of the clan.
*Colin Campbell of Glenure	*male	Known as the Red Fox, a Campbell who has been appointed King's factor on Appin lands. He is described as having red hair and being a bitter enemy of the Stewart clan, who seeks to drive out loyal Stewart tenants.
*Sheamus	*male	A nickname for James Stewart, who is described as Ardshiel's agent and the person who manages the collection of rents from the clan members.
