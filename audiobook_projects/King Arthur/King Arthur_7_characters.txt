King Arthur	male	The sovereign king of Britain, tall and strong, known for his knightly virtues. He is betrayed by Morgan le Fay into a false battle with Sir Accolon, who uses a counterfeit sword to wound him severely. He ultimately reveals his identity and defeats Sir Accolon, showing mercy despite the betrayal.
*King Urience	male	Arthur's brother and fellow king, who is also captured by Morgan le Fay's enchantments. He appears briefly in the story as one of the knights who follows Arthur on a hunt.
Sir Accolon of Gaul	male	A knight from Gaul who is tricked by Morgan le Fay into fighting King Arthur. He becomes a traitor, believing he's serving his sister Morgan le Fay in her plot to kill Arthur. He reveals himself as a loyal knight of Arthur's court after the battle.
Sir Damas	male	A treacherous knight who is the antagonist of this chapter. He imprisons knights and forces them to fight his brother Outzlake, or face death. He is the false knight who tricks Arthur into a battle with Accolon.
*Sir Outzlake	male	The noble brother of Sir Damas, who is disabled by wounds. He is the rightful heir to their father's inheritance and fights for his rights against his treacherous brother. He is wounded in battle but shows great honor.
*Morgan le Fay	*female	The great enchantress and half-sister of King Arthur. She is the mastermind behind the deception that leads to Arthur's near-death battle with Accolon, using magic and false promises of power.
*Vivien	*female	The lady of the lake who loves King Arthur and intervenes to save him from his own sword. She is a powerful enchantress who helps Arthur by revealing the truth about the false sword.
Damsel of Morgan le Fay	*female	A false damsel who claims to be a servant of Morgan le Fay but is actually one of her agents. She deceives Arthur with false promises and later reveals herself to be a witch who is burned by the cursed mantle.
Dwarf	*male	A servant of Morgan le Fay who delivers Excalibur to Sir Accolon. He is part of the enchantment that leads to the battle between Arthur and Accolon.
