King Arthur	male	Emperor of Rome, descended from Belinus and Brennius, son of Helen, claiming right to Roman Empire through ancestry. He is the central figure who refuses tribute from Rome and leads military campaigns against Lucius Tiberius. He is described as manly-seeming, strong enough to keep his kingdoms without paying tribute, and ultimately crowned Emperor in Rome.
Lucius Tiberius	male	Emperor of Rome who demands tribute and homage from King Arthur. He is the primary antagonist, leading a massive army against Britain, ultimately defeated and killed by King Arthur in battle at Soissons. He is described as angry when ambassadors return with refusal to submit.
Sir Cador of Cornwall	male	Knight of the Round Table who speaks first at the council, advocating for war against Rome. He is chosen to convey King Arthur's message to the Roman ambassadors and later sent to escort them out of Britain.
King Anguish of Scotland	male	Scottish king who swears to avenge Rome's past oppression and promises twenty thousand men-at-arms to King Arthur.
*King of Little Britain	*male	A king who promises thirty thousand men to King Arthur's cause.
*Sir Lancelot	male	Knight of the Round Table who promises a great host to King Arthur.
Sir Gawain	male	Knight of the Round Table, nephew to King Arthur. He is sent to confront Emperor Lucius and engages in combat with a Tuscan knight named Prianius. He is wounded but healed by the balm of Paradise.
Sir Bors	male	Knight of the Round Table who accompanies Sir Gawain in confronting Emperor Lucius. He fights valiantly against Romans and is captured but later freed by Sir Gawain.
*Sir Lionel	male	Knight of the Round Table who fights alongside Sir Bors and Sir Bedivere against Roman forces.
*Sir Bedivere	male	Knight of the Round Table who fights alongside Sir Lionel and Sir Bors against Romans.
