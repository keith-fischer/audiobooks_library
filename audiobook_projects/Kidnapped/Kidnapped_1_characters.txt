David Balfour	*male	The protagonist of the story, a 17-year-old boy leaving his father's house in Essendean to journey to the House of Shaws. He is described as a steady, canny lad who has lived in a quiet country setting and is eager to explore the world beyond his familiar surroundings. He carries a letter from his deceased father that directs him to the House of Shaws and contains gifts from Mr. Campbell.
Mr. Campbell	*male	The minister of Essendean who is deeply attached to David and sees him off on his journey. He is described as a good, Christian man who took pleasure in having David's father visit the manse. He gives David a letter from his late father, along with gifts including money and a recipe for Lilly of the Valley water. He advises David on how to behave in his new environment and expresses deep affection for him.
*Ebenezer Balfour	*male	The father of David Balfour, who is deceased. He was a country dominie and man of learning who had connections to the House of Shaws. He left David a letter directing him to go to the House of Shaws and gave Mr. Campbell instructions about delivering it after his death.
*Mrs. Campbell	*female	The wife of Mr. Campbell who, along with her husband, gives David gifts upon his departure. She is part of the family that shows affection for David and helps prepare him for his journey to the House of Shaws.
