Alan Breck	male	A Stewart clan member, Alan is a loyal and courageous friend who has helped the narrator through difficult times. He is proud, quick to anger but also capable of deep remorse and kindness. Despite his initial misdeed of taking the narrator's money, he shows genuine care for him during their perilous journey through the Highlands. He is described as having a 'set countenance' and being 'blithe to sponge upon the money he had driven me to beg.' He is also shown to be sensitive about his family's history and political stance, having been on the royal side during the Battle of Preston Pans.
David	male	The narrator of the story, David is a proud and angry man who has been betrayed by his friend Alan. He suffers from physical weakness due to illness, and is described as having a 'sore throat,' 'painful stitch in his side,' and being 'deadly sick.' He is unforgiving by nature, slow to take offense but slower to forget it. His pride and anger lead him to insult Alan repeatedly, even when he is dying. He shows moments of vulnerability and self-reflection, particularly when he realizes his own cruelty.
