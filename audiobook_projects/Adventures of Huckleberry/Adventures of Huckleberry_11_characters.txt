Sarah Williams	female	A young woman who claims to be traveling from Hookerville to find her uncle Abner Moore in St. Petersburg. She is described as tired and weary from walking seven miles, and appears to be a runaway apprentice who has been treated badly by her master. She is initially suspicious of the woman she meets and lies about her identity, but eventually reveals that she has been traveling to Goshen instead of St. Petersburg.

*Abner Moore	*male	Sarah's uncle who lives at the upper end of town. He is mentioned as someone she hopes to find for help when her mother is sick and out of money.
*Huck Finn	*male	A boy who was murdered, the subject of discussion in the chapter. His death is connected to a reward being offered for his killer, and there's speculation about who might have done it.
*Pap	*male	Huck Finn's father, described as a hard lot who treated Huck badly. He is mentioned in connection with the murder of his son and possible involvement in a scheme to get Huck's money.
*Jim	*male	A runaway slave who is hiding on Jackson's Island. He is described as being asleep in a cavern when the narrator finds him, and he works quickly to prepare their escape after learning they are being hunted.
*Judge Thatcher	*male	A judge who is mentioned in connection with giving money to Pap for hunting the runaway slave Jim.
Mrs. Judith Loftus	female	The woman who Sarah encounters in the chapter. She is described as a suspicious and observant person who figures out that Sarah is actually a boy named George Peters. She gives advice to Sarah and offers her protection.
*George Peters	*male	The real name of the person who Sarah claims to be. He is a runaway apprentice from the country who has been treated badly by his master.
*Elexander Peters	*male	A name that Sarah claims to be her real name, which she uses in a lie to the woman who is questioning her identity.
