Huckleberry	male	The narrator of the story, a young boy who attends school and lives with the Widow Douglas. He is learning to read, write, and do basic math but dislikes school. He has a habit of playing hooky when tired and is getting used to the widow's ways.
Miss Watson	female	The widow's sister who lives with the Widow Douglas and runs the household. She is strict about manners and education, scolding Huckleberry for making a mess with salt.
Widow Douglas	female	A kind woman who takes in Huckleberry and tries to civilize him. She is patient with his progress and proud of his improvement.
Judge Thatcher	male	A local judge who manages Huckleberry's money. He is surprised by Huckleberry's request to give him all his property and eventually buys it from him.
Jim	male	Miss Watson's enslaved man who possesses a hair-ball with magical properties. He is superstitious and believes the hair-ball can predict the future.
*Pap	*male	Huckleberry's father who has returned unexpectedly. He is described as a drunkard and an abusive parent, with a history of leaving Huckleberry.
