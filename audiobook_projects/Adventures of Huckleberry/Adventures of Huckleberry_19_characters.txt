Jim	*male	A enslaved man who travels with Huck on the raft, showing loyalty and compassion. He is described as having 'eyes bugged out' when hearing the duke's claim to nobility, and shows pity for both the duke and king.
Huck	*male	The narrator of the story, a young boy who travels down the Mississippi River with Jim. He is practical and observant, recognizing that the men claiming to be royalty are frauds but chooses not to expose them.
Baldhead	*male	An older man who claims to be the late Dauphin, son of Louis XVI and Marie Antoinette. He wears a 'bald head' and 'very gray whiskers', dressed in 'greasy blue woollen shirt' and 'ragged old blue jeans britches'. He is described as having a 'pore broken heart' and claiming to be the rightful King of France.
Bilgewater	*male	A younger man who claims to be the Duke of Bridgewater, a title he says comes from his great-grandfather. He is described as 'about thirty' and dressed in a 'greasy blue woollen shirt', 'ragged old blue jeans britches' and an 'old long-tailed blue jeans coat'. He has a 'pore broken heart' and claims to have been degraded by the world.
The Duke of Bridgewater	*male	A fraudulent claimant to the title of Duke of Bridgewater, who is actually a con man. He claims descent from an infant duke and was 'snaked down wrongfully out'n a high place'. He is described as wearing blue jeans and being 'ragged, worn, heart-broken' and 'degraded to the companionship of felons on a raft'.
The King	*male	A fraudulent claimant to the title of King of France, who claims to be Louis XVII. He is described as being 'at his age' and having 'gray hairs' and 'premature balditude'. He claims to be the 'wanderin', exiled, trampled-on, and sufferin' rightful King of France'.
