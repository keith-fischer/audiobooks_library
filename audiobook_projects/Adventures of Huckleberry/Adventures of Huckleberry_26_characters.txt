Mary Jane	female	The owner of the house where the king, duke, and narrator are staying. She is hosting a large supper for family members and guests. She has a room that she offers to the duke, and she is described as being at the head of the supper table. She is portrayed as kind-hearted but easily influenced by others.
*Susan	female	Mary Jane's sister who joins in the conversation with Hare-lip and helps to mediate between her and the narrator. She is described as being present at the supper table and later intervenes to help calm Hare-lip's anger.
Hare-lip	female	A girl who questions the narrator about England and his supposed familiarity with English customs. She is portrayed as curious but easily convinced of falsehoods, though she becomes suspicious when the narrator tells lies. She is described as being treated harshly by Mary Jane and Susan.
The King	*male	A fraud who claims to be a king and is staying with Mary Jane's family. He is described as being in charge of the group, making decisions about where to stay and what to do. He is portrayed as manipulative and self-serving, planning to steal money from the family.
The Duke	*male	A fraud who claims to be a duke and is staying with Mary Jane's family. He works alongside the king in their schemes, often disagreeing with the king but eventually following his lead. He is portrayed as greedy and willing to abandon their plans if they don't get enough money.
*Joanna	female	The same person as Hare-lip, who is referred to by her full name in one instance. She is a curious girl who questions the narrator about England and his supposed familiarity with English customs.
*The Doctor	*male	A doctor who is present in town and is causing concern for the frauds. He is described as being a threat to their plans, though his exact role in the story is unclear.
