Miss Watson	female	A strict religious woman who scolds Huck for his clothes and behavior. She prays with Huck in the closet but doesn't seem to have much success with his spiritual development. She believes in organized religion and the concept of 'Providence' but is contradicted by the widow's more compassionate interpretation.
*The Widow	female	A kind woman who takes care of Huck and tries to civilize him. She doesn't scold him like Miss Watson but instead cleans his grease and clay off, showing concern for his well-being. She believes in the concept of 'Providence' and teaches Huck about helping others.
*Pap	*male	Huck's father, a drunkard who beats him when sober. He hasn't been seen for over a year and is described as having long hair, being ragged, and resembling the drowned man found in the river. Huck is relieved that Pap has been gone but later becomes uncomfortable when he realizes it might not be his father.
Tom Sawyer	male	A boy who leads the gang activities and tells elaborate stories about robberies, Spanish merchants, elephants, camels, and genies. He is imaginative but believes in fantastical tales like Don Quixote and thinks Huck is ignorant for not believing his stories.
*Ben Rogers	*male	A boy who participates in the gang activities and gets a rag doll from one of their raids on a Sunday school picnic.
*Jo Harper	*male	A boy who participates in the gang activities and gets a hymn book and tract from one of their raids on a Sunday school picnic.
*Deacon Winn	*male	A man mentioned by Huck as someone who lost money on pork, used to illustrate that prayer doesn't always work.
*The Sunday School Teacher	*male	An authority figure who chases the boys after they disrupt a Sunday school picnic and charges in to stop them.
