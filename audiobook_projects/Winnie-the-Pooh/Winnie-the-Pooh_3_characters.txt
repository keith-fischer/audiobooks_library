Piglet	male	Lives in a house in the middle of a beech-tree in the forest. Has a grandfather named Trespassers W (short for Trespassers William). Enjoys telling stories about his grandfather's experiences and is easily frightened. Goes hunting with Pooh but quickly decides to leave when he realizes the situation might be dangerous.
Winnie-the-Pooh	male	A bear who believes he is hunting a Woozle. Thinks in a simple, innocent way and gets easily distracted by thoughts of his grandfather or Christopher Robin. Follows tracks but becomes confused when more animals appear, eventually realizing he was mistaken about the situation.
Christopher Robin	*male	A young boy who is friends with Pooh and Piglet. Comes down from a tree to find Pooh, scolds him for his foolish behavior, and comforts him. He is a source of reassurance and practicality in the story.
*Trespassers W	*male	Piglet's grandfather, whose name is inscribed on a sign near his house. Has two names: Trespassers and William, with the first being short for Trespassers Will. Known to have suffered from stiffness and shortness of breath in later years.
